Duckworth Lewis Score Calculator

Welcome to our website's DLS Score Calculator!

In the game of cricket, weather conditions can often disrupt matches, especially in limited-overs formats. To ensure fairness and maintain competitive spirit, the Duckworth Lewis Stern (DLS) method is employed to adjust target scores in rain-affected matches. Our DLS Score Calculator provides a reliable solution for determining the revised target score.

Our calculator covers various scenarios that may arise due to rain interruptions during the match:

  1. Scenario 1: First Innings completed, but the start of the second innings is delayed. Resources reduced at the start of the innings.
  2. Scenario 2: First Innings completed, but the start of the second innings is curtailed. Resources reduced at the end of the innings.
  3. Scenario 3: First Innings completed, but the start of the second innings is interrupted, resulting in a reduction in overs. Resources reduced in the middle of the innings.
  4. Scenario 4: First innings is curtailed. Resources reduced at the end of the innings.
  5. Scenario 5: First innings is interrupted in the middle. Resources reduced in the middle of the innings.

By providing the relevant information in the respective forms, our calculator will apply the DLS method to calculate the revised target score accurately. This ensures that you can enjoy a fair and competitive match, even when unpredictable weather conditions come into play.

Simply complete the form with the necessary details, and our calculator will handle the rest, providing you with an accurate DLS score that reflects the revised target for the second innings. Experience the excitement of cricket without worrying about the impact of rain on the game with our DLS Score Calculator!

Calculate DLS Par Score for Curtailed First Innings (Cutshort)

The form below is used to calculate the Duckworth-Lewis-Stern (DLS) Par Score in a scenario where the first innings of a cricket match is Cutshort in the middle due to rain or other adverse conditions.

This situation arises when the team batting first is playing their innings. If rain or another interruption occurs, causing a significant delay, the play is stopped. If the duration of the stoppage exceeds the maximum resumable time for the first innings, then the first innings will be cut short. Following the interruption, Team Two will proceed with the second innings, either with the same number of overs as Team One batted or with reduced overs based on the stoppage time.

Consequently, the target score for Team Two will be adjusted based on the resources available to Team One during the cut short, as well as the overs allotted to Team Two. The DLS Par Score calculation takes these factors into account.
